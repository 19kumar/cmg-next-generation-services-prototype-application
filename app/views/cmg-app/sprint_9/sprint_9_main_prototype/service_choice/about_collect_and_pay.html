How child maintenance is paid

Collect and pay

IIf a Direct Pay arrangement does not work, the Child Maintenance Service may be able to collect the amount from the paying parent and pass it to the receiving parent. This is called Collect and Pay.

The Child Maintenance Service will:

  • calculate the amount of maintenance that must be paid, and tell both parents
  • collect payments from the paying parent and pass them on to the receiving parent, for a fee
  • make a new calculation each year, or if either parent’s circumstances change

There are on-going charges for a Collect and Pay arrangement.

Amount you pay Example
If you receive child maintenance 4% taken from each payment You'd lose £4 from a £100 payment
If you pay child maintenance 20% on top of each payment You'd pay £20 on top of a £100 payment

Collect and Pay can only be used if the other parent either:

  • refuses to respond to us
  • has missed previous payments (and there is evidence of this), or
  • agrees to use Collect and Pay
