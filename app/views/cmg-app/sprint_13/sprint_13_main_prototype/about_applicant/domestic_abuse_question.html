What is domestic abuse

Domestic abuse is any incident or pattern of incidents of controlling, coercive or threatening behaviour, violence or abuse. It is very common.

Domestic abuse can include, but is not limited to the following types of abuse:

  • psychological or emotional
  • physical or sexual
  • financial or economic
  • coercive control (an act or pattern of acts of assault, threats, humiliation, intimidation, degradation, isolation and control)
  • harassment and stalking
  • online or digital abuse

In the majority of cases, the abuser is a partner or ex-partner, but they can also be a family member or carer.
