UR insights: Users not fully aware they can enter partial info about other parents. Some people expressed real concern about entering financial info such as credit or debit cards. Sprint 2 11 April 2019 - 24 April 2019

'Maintenance liability order' moved to front and broken up, addition of task list, questions grouped, addition of 'consent order' in children section.

UR sessions: Bury jobcentre (3 interviews), call listening at Tyneview Park

UR insights: Collect and Pay is thought be only payment option; people change their mind back to Direct Pay after learning that no contact with the other parent is needed. We know we need to probe for partial info on other parent.
Sprint 3 24 April 2019 - 8 May 2019

Contact change to 'What is your phone number?', addition of 'Can we send text messages?', addition of 'Do you have an email address?', addition of 'How many children?', addition of 'other parent introduction', addition of other parent filter questions and Yes / No, Other parent information page.

UR sessions: Hyde, Stanley, North Shields jobcentres, Womensaid focus group, pre-recorded call listening on domestic abuse

UR insights: Perception of Collect and Pay being only option, incorrect advice given by Citizens Advice, Relate etc. Direct Pay not clear for those who cannot discuss arrangements without conflict, Direct Pay only viable for people with reassurance about back-up and support, economic control a common form of domestic abuse. Fear stops people using the service - fear of other parent having bank details, of things 'kicking off', of payments equalling child access. The domestic abuse questions - 'get them out of the way', have a softer lead-in, people often do not recognise the signs of domestic abuse - try 'these bahaviours'. Still a save and return expectation.

Sprint 4 9 May 2019 - 22 May 2019

Eligibility questions at front of application, Declartion at front, update to design of 'Domestic abuse', removal of report domestic abuse dropout, addition of 'Correspondence address', addition of 'Contact with other parent' question.

UR sessions: South Shields, Stanley jobcentres

UR insights: Improved understanding of the different payment types. It's now easier to tell us about the contact situation, once that's done people could focus on making a choice between payment types. More info entered about other parent. Still answering no to do you know about other parent's address, but adding more info to other questions including employment. Next steps page: unanswered questions around process, timescales, formats, domestic abuse - not recognising it, not realising what 'reporting' means, early question not a problem but it may be jarring to some.

Sprint 10 1 August 2019 - 14 August 2019

UR sessions: Citizens Advice Newcastle library, Family Gateway

UR insights: Not everyone has a phone, often need to enter someone else's number. Shared care question confusing. Online account: some have no access to internet or low digital skills. People are okay with it, but PIN and password really hard for them. People are expecting to have to reset passwords. Direct Pay description helpful (after Direct Pay question given its own page) - back-up and support content helpful. 'We have to tell the other parent': this is confusing. Domestic abuse advice on interrupt cards well received. Next steps: 'how long will it take?'

Sprint 11 15 August 2019 - 28 August 2019

UR sessions: Newcastle Integrated Domestic Abuse Service (NIDAS), Citizens Advice Hammersmith and Shepherd's Bush, North Shields jobcentre

UR insights: Domestic abuse info up front does not improve experiecne because the questions are still at end. Phone questions are okay, low income users canot afford phone or internet. Online accounts useful for many but it can pose a problem. Password resets are expected. Â£20 application fee makes it impossible for low income people to use service. Domestic abuse dropout at end felt like a 'dark pattern'.
Sprint 12 29 August 2019 - 11 Sept

UR sessions: Galashiels jobcentre (11 interviews: 3 end to end, 1 up to payment)

UR insights: Fee a problem, strong objections. Other parent info: would give up, seems like they need all of it not just some (x4 said this). Access to account: 'would have to drag kids down to library'. Don't need contact with the other parent: 'really good info up front'. Domestic abuse interrupt: too far in, but content really good. Court order: 'what's that?'. Phone number: fine, but not everyone has freely available phone. Address fine. Shared care confusing. Direct pay: can be conflated with direct debit. Payment types: distrust of other parent, content clear though. Bank details: 'no' to 'Do you need us to pass on...' â€“ misunderstanding around process we describe. Understanding table a struggle, particularly 'if you pay' content. Other parent questions fine. PIN and password - not playing well with people. Domestic abuse warm-up page: should be at front, not end, worry about what this might mean in the future (courts etc) - is it recorded? 'Reported': too formal, would have to think, lots of to and fro over this. Next steps: more useful, no further questions on this one.
Version 13 12 September 2019 - 25 sept

UR sessions: Killingworth jobcentre, 1:1 interviews on domestic abuse(2)

UR insights: Domestic abuse question at the end can feel repetitive of info at front of the journey - too many questions about it, 'just ask it at the front'. Low levels of trust with other parent makes people reluctant to share bank details. It's pushing people towards Collect and Pay. Universal Credit users familiar with accounts but some object due to safety concerns. PIN and passwords really problematic.
Version 14 26 September 2019 - 9 October 2019

UR sessions: Bristol jobcentre

UR insights: Fees a barrier. Bank accounts: not everyone has one. Domestic abuse: descriptions helped, reassurance not always strong enough. Data input overall okay. Shared care: complicated, what if separation is brand new? Payment types: generally clear, passing on to other parent a problem for fear of misuse. Collect and Pay seemed clear because people feel like DWP is acting as a third party, charges make people pause. Non-id phone numbers won't be answered. Online account is attractive to users, but in Bristol high level of asylum seekers who need help to do stuff online. PIN and password - 7-digit PIN missed, mix of letters and numbers, save and return assumption: 'Am I creating an account to come back to here?'
Version 15 10 October 2019 - 23 October 2019

UR sessions: Womensaid 1:1 interviews (3)

UR insights: Start - helpful that fee is waived, but not eeryone recognises domestic abuse. 'Tell someone about it' - 'what does this mean?'' but everyine clicked on it. (gov.uk page felt scary but also expected - 999, Womensaid England needs to be on there, navigating back sometimes a problem). Reassurance - 'If you're worried...' helpful and empathetic. Court decision - all 3 had to reread h1 text, really long. Back links 'really nice' - easily found. Domestic abuse - content really helpful in helping people recognise it. Reassurance messaging all inset text now - working well. Domesitc abuse in relation to who? Other parent? Reporting - reassurance again good, one user needed more. Examples in hint text - support worker not recognised as Womensaid. From 'behaviours' to 'domestic abuse' - catches people out. Reassurance - 'thumbs up for this'. Shared care - still confusing if a complex situation. Payment types - Direct Pay straightforward, back-up really good, 'love' what happens if it deosn't work. Bank details - still scary for some. Account online - reassurance, does other parent see the same account? If no to account, then what? How do we communicate? Next steps - what happens next, time frame? Numbers good, but how exactly will this communication happen.
